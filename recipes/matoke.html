Matoke

Description

Matoke are green, commonly used in East Africa. Matoke can be boiled , steamed, fried or stewed, and slightly hane a tangy flavor and firm texture when cooked.

Ingredients

  • 6-10 plantains (matoke)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • salt and hot pepper
  • water

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the plantains and cutting them int 1-inch thick slices.
  2. in a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic. saute them until they become translucent and fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and bell peppers to the pot. Cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes start to soften.
  4. sprinkle in the curry powder, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine theh spices with the vegetables.
  5. Add the sliced plantains to the pot and stir them gently to coat them with the seasoned vegetables.
  6. Pour enough water into the pot almost cover the plantains. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 20-30 minutes. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to prevent sticking.
  7. Simmer until the plantains are tender and have absorbed the flavors of the spices and vegetables. They should be soft but mushy.
  8. Once cooked, remove from heat and let it cool slightly. Serve your matoke hot garnished with fresh cilantro if desired.
